# Template Styles Reference

Three approved email template styles. Pick the one that fits the email's job — not the one that feels "fancier" or has been used most recently. Variety across a campaign keeps the inbox interesting.

---

## 1. Banner + Text + Products

**What it is:** Hero banner image at top, body copy in the middle, product grid at the bottom (typically 2–4 products in a row).

**Use it for:**
- Themed campaign emails 1 and 3 (Launch, Proof)
- Promo emails 3, 4, 5 (Sale Products, Category Sale, Best Sellers)
- Any email where the customer needs to see products to act

**Why it works:** Marries a visual hook with browsable options. Reduces decision friction by showing the actual SKUs.

**Anti-pattern:** Don't dump 8+ products in. If you can't pick 4, the email's theme isn't tight enough.

**Image brief checklist for the banner:**
- Hero subject (product, scene, person)
- Aspect ratio (typically 1.5:1 or 2:1 for desktop, mobile-aware)
- Text overlay or no text overlay
- Mood (warm/clean/playful/premium)
- What's explicitly *not* in it (e.g., "no coffee pods visible", "no plastic bottles")

---

## 2. Text Only

**What it is:** No banner. No product grid. Just copy and possibly a single CTA button. Often styled like a letter or personal note.

**Use it for:**
- Themed campaign email 2 (Educate / Inform)
- Promo email 7 (Last Chance — raw urgency)
- "A note from the founder" moments
- Any email where the *voice* is the value

**Why it works:** Cuts through inbox noise (most emails are designed). Feels personal. Lets the writing carry the weight. Often higher click rates because the single link/button gets all the attention.

**Anti-pattern:**
- Don't bury the CTA at the bottom of 12 paragraphs. Even text-only emails benefit from one obvious link.
- Don't fake "text only" with a stripped template that's still clearly templated. Either commit to plain-text feel or use a designed template.
- Don't use this for promo launches — you need the banner to communicate the offer instantly.

**Format guidance:**
- Sign off with a real name (the founder, or "The [Brand] Team")
- 2–5 short paragraphs typically; longer is fine if the story earns it
- One CTA, in-line or as a button

---

## 3. Banner + CTA + Text

**What it is:** One big banner image, one prominent CTA button right under it, then supporting copy below. Sometimes called the "one-button email".

**Use it for:**
- Themed campaign email 4 (Product & Offer)
- Promo emails 1, 2, 6 (Early Access, Launch, Ends Soon)
- Any email where one clear action wins

**Why it works:** Brutal clarity. The customer sees the offer, sees the button, clicks. No browsing. Highest click-through rate when the offer is strong.

**Anti-pattern:**
- Don't use it when the customer needs to compare products to decide.
- Don't put multiple CTAs in. One button. Period.
- Don't let the banner do too much explaining — banners with 30 words of text on them get ignored. Banner shows; copy below tells.

**Image brief checklist for the banner:**
- Single hero element (product or offer)
- Sale price / offer visible in the banner if it's a promo
- High contrast — must read on mobile
- Reasonable file size (Klaviyo emails should load fast)

---

## Choosing between templates — quick decision tree

```
Is this an Early Access / Launch / Ends Soon promo email?
  → Banner + CTA + text

Is this an "educate" or "personal note" email?
  → Text only

Does the customer need to see products to choose?
  → Banner + text + products

Default if unsure?
  → Banner + text + products (most versatile)
```

## Avoiding "template fatigue"

If three emails in a row use Banner + text + products, the inbox feels repetitive. Mix template styles across a 4-email or 7-email arc — for example:

- 4-email themed: Banner+text+products → Text only → Banner+text+products → Banner+CTA+text
- 7-email promo: Banner+CTA+text → Banner+CTA+text → Banner+text+products → Banner+text+products → Banner+text+products → Banner+CTA+text → Text only

---

## Mobile-first reminders

- ~60% of opens are on mobile. Banners must read at 320px wide.
- Single-column layouts always. No side-by-side text-image blocks that break.
- CTA buttons large enough to tap (minimum 44px height).
- Subject line + preview text are 80% of the open decision — design starts there.
