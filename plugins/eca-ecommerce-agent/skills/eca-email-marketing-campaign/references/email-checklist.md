# Pre-Send Email Checklist

Apply this to every campaign before declaring it ready. If anything's marked No, fix it before sending the plan to the user as a finished deliverable.

---

## Campaign-level checks

- [ ] **Famous-for line** — can be stated in 5 words or fewer
- [ ] **Topic type identified** — Category / Launch / Calendar / Promo
- [ ] **Not duplicate** — hasn't been run in the last 6–8 weeks (Klaviyo history checked)
- [ ] **Not back-to-back promo** — if this is a promo, the previous promo was 2–3+ weeks ago
- [ ] **Calendar sense** — send dates don't conflict with public holidays, major retail events, or another planned campaign
- [ ] **Cadence appropriate** — 2/week for themed series, compressed for promo series
- [ ] **Inventory check** — featured SKUs are in stock through the campaign window

## Per-email checks

For every single email in the campaign:

- [ ] **Subject line** — 3 variants, < 50 chars where possible, no clickbait
- [ ] **Preview text** — complements subject, doesn't repeat it
- [ ] **Template style** — matches the email's job (use decision tree in `template-styles.md`)
- [ ] **Hero image** — briefed specifically (subject, mood, what's NOT in it)
- [ ] **Body copy** — on-brand voice (cross-check against brand-intelligence)
- [ ] **Single primary CTA** — one button, one main action
- [ ] **Standalone test** — works as a reader's first and only exposure
- [ ] **No phrases on brand "avoid" list** — no "introducing...", no "elevate", no "revolutionary"
- [ ] **Mobile-friendly** — copy reads at 320px wide, CTA tappable
- [ ] **Offer matches reality** — if discount mentioned, Shopify code is set up and end-date matches
- [ ] **Links target the right page** — product, collection, or landing page exists

## Voice cross-check (from brand-intelligence)

Quick gut-check questions before locking copy:

- Would the founder read this aloud without wincing?
- Does it contain any phrases on the avoid list?
- Is it doing the brand's job (selling, connecting, differentiating) — or just describing the product?
- Is it warm without being saccharine, confident without being smug?

## Standalone test (the most important check)

For each email in the series, imagine a reader whose **first ever email from the brand** is *this* email. Ask:

1. Do they understand what we sell within 3 seconds of opening?
2. Do they understand why this email matters (the hook)?
3. Do they have a clear next action (the CTA)?
4. Would they buy or click from this email alone?

If any answer is no, rewrite. The email cannot rely on the previous one having been read.

## Cadence sanity (across the quarter)

Step back and look at the campaign in context:

- [ ] Doesn't run during a planned product launch or BFCM blackout
- [ ] Send days line up with historical best-performing windows
- [ ] Mixes template styles across the arc (not 4 banner-text-product emails in a row)
- [ ] Mixes subject line styles (curiosity, benefit, specificity, urgency)

## After send — track and feed back

Once the campaign runs, capture:

- Per-email: open rate, click rate, revenue per recipient
- Best-performing subject line (for the A/B variants)
- Any unsubscribes spike on a specific email
- Customer reply themes (compliments, complaints, questions)

Feed this back into the next campaign planning session — it's how the topic-selection step gets sharper over time.
