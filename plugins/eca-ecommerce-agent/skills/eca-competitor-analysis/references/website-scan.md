# Website Scan Checklist

What to pull from a competitor's site, and where to find it. The aim is to leave with enough to (a) describe how they position and price, and (b) spot what they've left open for us.

## Pages to hit (in order)

1. **Homepage** — the hero is where they stake their positioning. Read above-the-fold first.
2. **A top product page (PDP)** — how they sell a single product: claims, benefits, proof, price.
3. **Pricing / bundles / shop-all** — full range and price ladder, multi-buy and bundle logic.
4. **Subscription page** (if any) — cadence, discount, lock-in, flexibility.
5. **About / "why us" / story** — founder narrative, differentiation, values, origin.
6. **Cart / checkout entry** — shipping thresholds, guarantees, payment options, urgency.
7. **The entry popup** — capture the first-order offer *before* dismissing it; it's the real acquisition hook.

## Tooling note

These are JavaScript-rendered storefronts. A plain fetch usually returns an empty shell or an "enable JavaScript" message — when you see that, switch to the Claude in Chrome browser tools (navigate, then read rendered page text) rather than retrying the fetch.

## What to extract

### Positioning & messaging
- **Primary hook** — the one-line promise in the hero.
- **Value props** — the 3–5 benefits they repeat across the site.
- **Tone of voice** — clinical? cheeky? premium? earnest? Quote a phrase that captures it.
- **Target customer** — who the copy and imagery are clearly speaking to (mark as a *read*, not a fact).
- **Proof & trust** — review counts/ratings, press logos, certifications, awards, UGC, guarantees, founder story.

### Pricing & offers
- **Range & price points** — cheapest to most expensive, and the "hero" price.
- **Unit economics signal** — price per unit/serving where shown (lets you compare like-for-like).
- **Bundles & multi-buy** — what they discount and by how much.
- **Subscription** — discount %, cadence, perks, how easy to cancel.
- **Live promo** — any sitewide or product discount running right now.
- **Shipping & returns** — free-shipping threshold, delivery speed, returns/refund/guarantee terms.
- **First-order incentive** — the popup offer (% off, free gift, free shipping for email/SMS signup).

## Capture observed vs inferred

Write prices, thresholds, and on-page claims as **facts**. Write audience and strategy interpretation as **reads**. The operator needs to trust the numbers and weigh the interpretation separately.
