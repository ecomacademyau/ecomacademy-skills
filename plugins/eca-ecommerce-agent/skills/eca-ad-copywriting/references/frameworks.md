# Strategic Frameworks — what makes the copy strategic, not random

The rules in `copy-rules.md` cover the mechanics. These five frameworks cover the *thinking*. Use them to decide what each headline and primary text should actually say.

---

## 1. Data-driven creativity

Blend creativity with testing logic — every line should be optimised for acquisition, and written so it can be tested against an alternative.

- Deliberately test **emotional hooks** vs **utility hooks** for the same product.
  - Emotional: *"Designed for the moments that matter."*
  - Utility: *"Save time and money on your daily essentials."*
- When you write the 10 headlines, make sure the set includes **both** emotional and utility angles so the operator can learn which the audience responds to.

## 2. Platform-specific (Meta) best practice

- Write for **feed, story, and reel** placements with a native, organic feel — ad copy should read like content, not a banner.
- Favour **video-first, short-form (6–15s)** concepts; the copy is the hook that earns the first 3 seconds.
- Keep formats in mind: single image, carousel, collection — the headline still does the heavy lifting.

## 3. Funnel stage — TOF / MOF / BOF

Match the angle to where the audience sits. Ask the user which stage this campaign targets; if unsure, default to TOF for cold acquisition.

- **Top of funnel (TOF)** — cold strangers. Lead with **emotion and curiosity**; use storytelling to introduce the brand.
  - *e.g. "Why 10,000+ people switched to this."*
- **Middle of funnel (MOF)** — they know you. Lead with **social proof** (reviews, testimonials, UGC) and **educate** on benefits/features.
  - *e.g. "Made for the perfect result — don't settle."*
- **Bottom of funnel (BOF)** — ready to buy. Drive **urgency and scarcity**.
  - *e.g. "Only a few left — claim yours today."*

## 4. Emotion + utility in the copy

Every strong ad pairs a feeling with a reason-to-believe.

- **Emotional storytelling** for connection: *"Feel good every single morning."*
- **Tangible utility** for the rational mind: *"Saves you 10 minutes a day."*
- **Clear CTA** matched to intent: *"Get yours today"* / *"Shop now for exclusive savings."*

Aim to land **both** within a single primary-text Version 2: emotion in the hook, utility in the bullets.

## 5. Urgency & exclusivity (FOMO) — for CTAs and BOF

Drive fear of missing out to prompt action, especially at BOF.

- **Urgency:** *"Ends tonight."* / *"Only 3 left."*
- **Exclusivity:** *"Limited edition — subscribers only."* / *"Early access for our list."*

Use honestly — only invoke scarcity/urgency that's real. False scarcity erodes trust and can breach ad policy.

---

## Putting it together

When you write the 10 headlines and 2 primary texts:
1. Decide the **funnel stage** (drives the dominant angle).
2. Spread the 10 headlines across **segments** and across **emotion vs utility** (framework 1 + 4).
3. Build Version 2 to carry **emotion in the hook + utility in the bullets** (framework 4).
4. Reserve **urgency/exclusivity** for the CTA and any BOF variants (framework 5).
5. Keep everything **native to Meta** and short enough to win the scroll (framework 2).
