# Analysis Framework — nine things to extract from the reviews

Run this over the full review corpus before writing any personas. For each section, pull **recurring themes** and **verbatim phrases** — the customer's own words are what make the output usable for copy. Sample across all star ratings, not just the 5-stars.

---

## 1. Customer benefits
- The top benefits customers actually mention (in their words).
- Recurring themes around health, lifestyle, convenience, or emotional well-being.
- Which benefits come up most often (frequency = priority for messaging).

## 2. Pain points & challenges
- What problem were customers experiencing **before** the product?
- What frustration or unmet need did the product solve?
- The "before state" — name it concretely, in their language.

## 3. Emotional drivers
- Which emotions are triggered consistently? (relief, excitement, confidence, pride, calm, belonging…)
- The language that signals emotional connection to the brand ("obsessed", "game-changer", "love").

## 4. Purchase motivations
- The specific reasons customers give for choosing this product.
- Any **trigger event** that led to the purchase (a life change, a frustration peak, a recommendation, a season).

## 5. Demographic insights
- Patterns in age, gender, location, lifestyle (only where the reviews actually reveal them).
- Where available: occupation, hobbies, family status.
- Mark inferred demographics as a *read*, not a fact.

## 6. Behavioural insights
- How customers describe **using** the product in daily life.
- Habits, rituals, frequency of use, time of day, use-cases (note multi-use mentions).

## 7. Customer segments → the personas
- Group customers into **3–5 distinct personas** by shared behaviour, motivation and need.
- Each persona must be genuinely distinct; merge overlaps.
- Fill the per-persona structure (see `templates/persona-template.md`): name & description, key benefits valued, primary pain points, emotional triggers, buying behaviour, preferred messaging tone/style, and a notable real review quote.

## 8. Content preferences
- Based on the tone of reviews, what content resonates — educational, emotional, humorous, aspirational?
- Preferred formats hinted at — short video, testimonials/UGC, infographics, how-to.
- Note differences **by segment** where they show up.

## 9. Objections & concerns
- Common objections in negative or neutral reviews (price, taste, shipping, doubt, comparison to alternatives).
- For each, suggest how to **address it in ad/email messaging** (the rebuttal angle).
- This section is the most actionable — don't skip it.

---

## Output of this analysis
The cross-cutting findings (1–6, 8, 9) become the **analysis summary** at the top of the report. Section 7 becomes the **personas**. Then add **key takeaways for marketing strategy** — the so-what: which benefits, language, and offers to lead with, and which objections to neutralise.
