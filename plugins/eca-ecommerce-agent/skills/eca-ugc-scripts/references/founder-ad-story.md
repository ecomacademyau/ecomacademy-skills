# Founder Ad Story
**Status: FULL (gold-standard reference).** A ~60-second founder monologue, shot on a phone, that builds trust with a cold audience by telling the true story of the moment the founder realised the customer's problem was real — and what they did about it.

> Source: the "Shoot Your Own Founder Ad" worksheet. This is the reference quality bar for every other type. Read `00-engine.md` first.

## Why it works
People keep watching for one reason: **it feels like a real person telling the truth, not a brand selling.** The viewer thinks **"that's me"** (you're describing my life), then **"I need that"** (your product is the way out). Start with "that's me" so they trust you; *earn* "I need that." Keep one honest line alive to the very end so it never tips into a pitch.

## Format rule — talking-to-camera ONLY (non-negotiable)
A founder ad is the founder talking **straight to camera in one continuous take**, exactly like a Story posted to their own feed. **Never use b-roll, cutaways, supered clips, or a second angle.** The entire power of this format is unbroken eye contact and the feeling that a real person is talking *to you* — the moment you cut away, it reads as an ad and the spell breaks. So: no `[b-roll]`/`[cutaway]` notes in this script, no edited inserts. The only thing on screen is the founder's face (and, in Beat 4, the product held up in their own hand — still in the same continuous shot, not a cutaway). On-screen captions are fine; cut-to footage is not.

## Step 1 — Generate the hook (0–3s)
Use the hook engine in `00-engine.md` with VIDEO TYPE = "Founder Monologue". Founder-specific hook rules:
- A personal, raw, slightly-vulnerable line. Lead with a **confession, a contrarian truth, or a small embarrassment**.
- Specific enough to stop the scroll, universal enough that 1,000 people feel "that's me."
- Never "Hi I'm" or "Have you ever." No price.

Generate 10, pick **3 to shoot** — safe / medium / one that makes the founder slightly uncomfortable. The numbers pick the winner.

## Step 2 — Pick the track
The hook tells you which story you're telling. Choose one; it changes three beats.
- **Track A — "My product fixes something that frustrated me."** The annoying problem, the thing that didn't work, the wasted money.
- **Track B — "My product gives a feeling people are missing."** The taste, the calm, the way it makes them feel — the thing nothing else delivered.

## Step 3 — The six beats
Work one beat at a time. Answer each out loud first, then write down exactly what was said — **don't tidy it up; the rough version is the good version.** Do not name the product until Beat 4.

| Beat | Name | ~Time | What to do |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **The hook** | 3s | One of the 3 chosen hooks. Say it flat — no smile, no "Hi I'm," no warm-up. |
| 2 | **Prove you lived it** | 5s | The one specific detail that proves this was really your life — real moment, real numbers. No product yet. (Track A: the messy detail showing the problem was real. Track B: the moment you realised everything out there felt cheap/fake/not-it.) This is where most founder ads die. |
| 3 | **What you worked out** | 10s | The penny-drop. The thing you finally understood that the customer hasn't — usually: it was never their fault, the right thing just didn't exist. |
| 4 | **What you made** | 15s | Now show the product by **lifting it into frame in the same shot** (not a cutaway). Plain words on what it does and how simple it is. The easy/fast part is the part they want. No feature lists. |
| 5 | **What changed** | 15s | What's different now — the **feeling, not the stat**; the moment you knew it worked. (Track A: what's different now the problem's gone. Track B: what it feels like now you finally have it.) |
| 6 | **The ask** | 10s | Talk to one person. Name their exact situation, then tell them to try it. Like a friend, not a salesperson. |

**The rhythm, in one line:** that's me → that's me → what I worked out → here's what I made → here's what changed → here's the ask.

## Step 4 — Stitch & check
Put the six lines in order. **Don't add joining words; don't smooth it.** If one seam feels jarring, that's the only place you may change anything. Read it aloud at normal pace — it should land near **60 seconds**. Running over? The fix is almost always Beat 4 (founders over-explain the product) — cut it to the steps. Sounds like a brand wrote it? Say the line again like you're talking to a friend, keep that version.

## Worked example (Track A — reusable desk lunch-warmer; shows the shape, don't copy)
- **Hook:** "I ate a cold lunch at my desk every day for two years and just accepted it."
- **Prove it:** "The microwave queue downstairs was ten people deep at noon, so I stopped bothering. Sad sandwich, every single day."
- **What I worked out:** "It wasn't that I didn't have time. There was just nothing that let me eat a proper hot meal without leaving my desk."
- **What I made:** "So I made this. You plug it in, drop your container in, and twenty minutes later it's hot. That's it."
- **What changed:** "First week I had hot leftovers at my desk and honestly felt like a functioning adult again."
- **The ask:** "If you're stuck eating cold lunches at work, try this. It's the only thing that fixed it for me."

Six lines, straight through = a full script. None of it sounds like an ad.

## Shooting notes
Phone, front camera, like you just turned it on, held at arm's length like a Story. **One continuous take, talking straight to camera — no b-roll, no cutaways, no edits.** No music, no ring light, no script in hand — talk like it's a voice note to a mate. The product only appears when you lift it into the same frame in Beat 4. Shoot it **three times, one per hook**, one take each. Talk from the heart.
