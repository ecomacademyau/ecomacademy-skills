# Blog Post Styles

Seven proven styles. Each ranks in Google **and** gets cited by AI engines when written to the structure below. Pick the style that matches the search intent you found in research — don't force a topic into the wrong shape.

Every style still obeys the universal rules in `seo-geo-checklist.md`: answer-first opening, question-style H2s, scannable structure, a table where it fits, an FAQ block, 2–4 product-insert cards, and a closing CTA.

---

## 1. Listicle ("Top 5…", "7 Ways to…")

**Best for:** broad, high-volume informational searches; quick wins; social-shareable.
**Search intent:** informational, often early-funnel.

**Structure:**
- Answer-first intro: state what the list delivers and the single most important takeaway up front.
- Numbered H2 for each item (`<h2>1. …</h2>`). Keep items parallel in length and depth.
- Each item: the point, why it matters, a concrete how/example. Insert a product card next to the 1–2 items the product most directly serves.
- Table summarising the list items where it fits (e.g., item vs benefit).
- FAQ block + closing CTA.

**Why it ranks / gets cited:** numbered lists are lifted verbatim into AI answers and featured snippets. Make each item self-contained so it can be quoted alone.

---

## 2. How-to / Educational

**Best for:** "how do I…", problem-solving, demonstrating expertise (E-E-A-T).
**Search intent:** informational, often mid-funnel (they have the problem now).

**Structure:**
- Answer-first intro: one or two sentences on the outcome and roughly how long/hard it is.
- Optional "What you'll need" list (a natural place for a product card).
- Numbered steps as H2s (`<h2>Step 1: …</h2>`), each with a clear action and the why.
- Tips / common mistakes section.
- The product as the thing that makes the method easier/faster — inserted at the step where it helps.
- FAQ block + closing CTA.

**Why it ranks / gets cited:** step structures map perfectly to "how to" AI answers and How-To rich results. Be specific and sequential.

---

## 3. Influencer / Authority Product Review

**Best for:** borrowing the search demand and trust around a well-known expert/figure in the niche (this is the example post style).
**Search intent:** informational → commercial; people searching the figure's name + a problem.

**Structure:**
- Hero image + answer-first intro that names the figure and the promise ("Here's what [Name] actually recommends, and how to apply it today").
- A "Who is [Name]?" credibility section.
- The figure's method broken into numbered tactics/principles (listicle-style H2s).
- Weave the product in where it embodies the method ("this is exactly why tools that … work — [Product] does this").
- Product-insert cards next to the relevant tactics; an "applying it today" closer that points to the products.
- FAQ block + strong dual-product CTA.

**Critical guardrails (do not skip):**
- Only reference **real public figures** factually. Use what is genuinely on the public record about their methods/philosophy.
- **Never fabricate quotes, endorsements, or partnerships.** Do not imply the figure endorses the brand or product. Frame as "applying [Name]'s widely-taught method", not "[Name] recommends [Product]".
- Avoid defamatory or false claims. If you can't support a statement about the person from the public record, don't make it.
- This protects the brand legally and keeps the content trustworthy (which is also what AI engines reward).

## 4. Comparison / "Best X for Y"

**Best for:** high commercial intent; buyers comparing options or deciding what to get.
**Search intent:** commercial investigation — closest to the sale of the informational styles.

**Two sub-shapes:**
- **"A vs B"** (e.g., "Liquid vs ground coffee") — head-to-head on the dimensions buyers care about.
- **"Best X for Y"** (e.g., "Best coffee for busy mornings") — a shortlist with a clear winner.

**Structure:**
- Answer-first intro that gives the verdict up front (the winner / who each option suits) — then justify it.
- **A comparison table** is mandatory here (options × the criteria that matter: price, speed, taste, effort, etc.). This is the single most-cited element in AI shopping answers.
- A section per option/criterion with honest pros and cons.
- A "which should you choose" decision section mapping buyer types to picks — your product as the best fit for the target buyer.
- Product-insert card(s) for the recommended pick; FAQ + CTA.

**Why it ranks / gets cited:** AI engines love synthesising comparisons and pull tables and verdicts straight into answers. Honesty (real pros/cons) increases citation trust and conversion.

---

## 5. Question / FAQ Answer-Style

**Best for:** owning a specific question outright (featured snippets + AI Overviews).
**Search intent:** informational, precise ("what is…", "why does…", "can you…", "how long…").

**Structure:**
- **The first 40–60 words must be a complete, direct answer** to the title question — formatted so it can stand alone (this is what wins the snippet/citation).
- Then expand: context, nuance, edge cases, related sub-questions as H2s.
- A table or bulleted breakdown where the answer has parts.
- Tie to the product where it's the practical answer to the underlying need.
- A larger FAQ block of adjacent questions; CTA.

**Why it ranks / gets cited:** this style is built for the snippet and the generative answer box. One question, fully and concisely answered, then deepened.

---

## 6. Ultimate Guide / Pillar

**Best for:** topical authority; becoming the definitive resource; earning links and internal-linking hub.
**Search intent:** broad informational; "complete guide to…", "everything about…".

**Structure:**
- Answer-first executive summary at the top (and ideally a "key takeaways" box) — long pages still need the answer early.
- Table of contents with anchor links (essential on long posts).
- Comprehensive H2 sections covering every facet, each able to stand alone; H3s beneath.
- Multiple tables/lists; embed or link out to credible data.
- Several internal links to your narrower posts (this is the hub of a content cluster) and 2–4 product cards placed where relevant.
- Big FAQ block; CTA.

**Why it ranks / gets cited:** depth and completeness signal authority to Google and give AI engines a rich, well-structured source to pull many answers from. Pair with the narrower styles that link up to it.

---

## 7. Myth-busting / Problem-solver

**Best for:** engagement, correcting misconceptions, and naturally positioning the product as the fix.
**Search intent:** informational with a frustration ("why does my … ", "is it true that …", "[problem] fix").

**Two sub-shapes:**
- **Myth-busting** ("5 myths about …") — state the myth, the truth, the evidence.
- **Problem diagnosis** ("Why your … and how to fix it") — symptom → cause → fix.

**Structure:**
- Answer-first intro naming the misconception/problem and the real story in one line.
- H2 per myth/cause. For myths: **Myth → Truth → Why**. For problems: **Symptom → Cause → Fix**.
- The product appears as the fix/the better way where honest.
- A summary table (myth vs truth, or cause vs fix); FAQ; CTA.

**Why it ranks / gets cited:** contrarian/corrective framing earns clicks and links; the clear myth→truth structure is highly quotable for AI engines answering "is it true that…".

## Choosing the style (quick guide)

| If the search looks like… | Use style |
|---|---|
| "top / best ways to / ideas for" | Listicle |
| "how to / how do I" | How-to |
| "[expert name] + topic" | Influencer/authority |
| "X vs Y" / "best X for Y" / comparing options | Comparison |
| "what is / why does / can / how long" (one tight question) | Question/FAQ |
| "complete guide / everything about" (broad) | Ultimate guide |
| "is it true / myths / why is my … not working" | Myth-busting/problem-solver |

When a topic could fit two styles, pick the one that matches the *buyer's stage* — comparison and influencer styles sit closest to the sale; listicle and guide sit earliest.
