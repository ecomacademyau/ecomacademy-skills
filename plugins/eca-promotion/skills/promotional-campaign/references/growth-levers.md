# The Promotion Builder — Ecommerce Growth Levers

Every promotion should start with **why** before **what**. The mistake most brands make is reaching for a tactic ("let's do 20% off") before deciding which growth lever the promo is actually meant to move. This framework forces that decision first, then maps the lever to the tactics that serve it.

Use this in **Stage 1 (intake)**: pick the lever, set the revenue goal, name the business goal(s), then choose an offer mechanic that the matrix says fits the lever. Confirm every input with the user — never assume.

## The four growth levers

Levers split across **Sales** (what happens once someone's on the site) and **Marketing** (how people get there / onto the list):

```
                 SALES
   Average Order Value  |  Conversion Rate
  ----------------------+----------------------
        Traffic         |     Database
                 MARKETING
```

| Lever | What it moves | Pick it when the goal is… |
|-------|---------------|---------------------------|
| **Conversion Rate** (Sales) | More of the existing traffic buys | Turn visits into orders; shift stock; react to an event |
| **Average Order Value** (Sales) | Each order is worth more | Lift basket size / margin without needing more traffic |
| **Traffic / Awareness** (Marketing) | More people arrive | Get in front of new audiences; build reach |
| **Database Growth** (Marketing) | More people on the list | Grow the email/SMS list you can remarket to for free |

## Tactics per lever (the matrix)

When the lever is chosen, propose offer mechanics / activities from its column — don't pull a tactic from a column you're not trying to move.

### Average Order Value (Sales)
- Buy 2 for $XX
- Spend & Save (spend $X, save $Y)
- Buy One Get One 50%
- Increase prices
- Spend to win
- VIP Program

### Conversion Rate (Sales)
- Site-wide discount
- Collection discount
- Flash sale
- Free shipping / express
- Gift with purchase
- Limited edition
- Markdowns

### Traffic / Awareness (Marketing)
- Google Ads
- Facebook Ads
- Search engine optimisation
- Video Ads
- Product reviews
- Affiliate marketing
- Public relations
- Offline marketing

### Database Growth (Marketing)
- On-site capture
- Off-site competitions
- Off-site sign-ups
- Partnerships
- In-store sign-up
- Event sign-ups
- Referral marketing

## How the lever shapes the campaign

The lever doesn't just pick the offer — it changes where the campaign's weight goes:

- **Conversion Rate** → the offer itself does the work. Full email/SMS arc + ads + on-site all pushing the discount. (This is the "classic sale" — EOFY, BFCM, flash sale.)
- **Average Order Value** → the *mechanic* matters more than the headline %. Lead with multi-buy / spend-thresholds; on-site and email should surface the threshold and progress to it; check that cart/upsell mechanics support it.
- **Traffic / Awareness** → ad spend and creative are the centre of gravity; the offer is the hook to justify the click. Weight Stage 4 (ads) heavily; the email arc may be lighter.
- **Database Growth** → the *capture mechanism* is the deliverable (competition, GWP for signing up, referral). Weight Stage 3's early-access/capture phase and the on-site capture, not the checkout discount. Success metric is signups, not just revenue.

A single campaign can have a primary and secondary lever (e.g. a Conversion-Rate sale that also runs a Database-Growth early-access capture). Name the **primary** lever so the brief knows where to put its weight, and confirm with the user.

## Mapping to business goals

The business goal tunes the offer and the targeting. Confirm which one(s) apply:

| Business goal | Tends toward | Notes |
|---------------|--------------|-------|
| **Clear stock** | Markdowns, collection discount, flash sale (Conversion) | Target the SKUs to move; watch margin floor |
| **Launch product** | Limited edition, gift-with-purchase, early access (Conversion + Database) | New-thing energy; seed reviews |
| **New customers** | Site-wide discount, first-order offer, traffic ads (Conversion + Traffic) | Acquisition cost matters; measure new-vs-returning |
| **Leverage event** | Whatever fits the calendar hook — EOFY, BFCM, Click Frenzy | The event *is* the reason; offer rides the occasion |
| **(custom)** | — | Capture the user's own goal verbatim |

## What the intake must produce

By the end of Stage 1 you should have, all **confirmed by the user** (no blanks, no guesses):

1. **Primary growth lever** (+ optional secondary)
2. **Revenue / outcome goal** ($ target, or signups for Database)
3. **Business goal(s)** — from the list above or custom
4. **The offer / promotion** — the mechanic, stated simply (the customer should be able to repeat it in five words)
5. **Conditions** — minimum spend, exclusions, "not in conjunction", returns policy, code vs automatic
6. **Promotion dates** — start and end date/time with timezone (and whether there's an early-access lead)

If any of these is missing or implied rather than stated, ask. This is the foundation the whole campaign is built on — a wrong assumption here propagates into every email, ad, and on-site change.
